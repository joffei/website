Allow me to tell you a little story.

Once Upon a Time... Not that kind of story

During my Junior year of High School, I had a study class with a very nice guy. Everyone knew him as the "Computer Geek". At this school, though, it has never been a bad thing to be a geek. In fact, geeks are the most popular. Every day, I saw him on his computer, typing what looked like an absolute mess.

"What's 'int'? What's with all the semicolons and braces? Why is the spacing so weird?"

Once he started talking to me about what he was doing, I was quikly hooked. However, it was anything but easy for me to start understanding all of this back-end work. I was stubborn though. I simply refused to let these machines get the better of me.

When the next year came along, there was one new class that caaught my attention: "Computer Science: Java." I didn't even have time to consider. My hand had already checked the box. In the first day of class, the teacher gave us this assignment: Write the instructions to make a peanutbutter and jelly sandwich...Everyone failed. Yet, somehow, I ended up loving it. Now I'm majoring in it.

By the time I left high school. I only knew one language strongly. Now I'm learning new languages whenever I can.
